Private Research Lab / MarketWatch

A market research desk that treats evidence as the product.

Manna-MarketWatch is a private local scanner and research lab for watchlists, manual snapshots, paper-trade journals, BTC replay studies, evidence attachments, and readiness checks.

The purpose is not to look like a trading app. The purpose is to make market thinking inspectable before any decision language gets trusted.

Current reality

MarketWatch has CLI, API, and a local Session Desk surface for refresh, recommendations, evidence, journal hygiene, BTC reports, and readiness checks.

Hard boundary

No exchange keys, no order routing, no wallets, no leverage, no live-money behavior, and no public financial advice.

What It Does

Research before action

Scanner Core

Tracks watch items, snapshots, alerts, scorecards, and local runtime reports from inspectable data.

Paper Lab

Stores named setup patterns, paper trades, evidence files, journal audits, and postmortems.

BTC Evidence Lane

Runs report-only candle, fee, spread, replay, readiness, and post-session review checks.

Public Boundary

What this page is not saying

Not Advice

Outputs are private research notes and discipline checks, not public recommendations.

Not Automation

No code path should place trades or simulate permission to place trades.

Not Certainty

Thin samples, stale data, and fee friction must block overconfident conclusions.

Not Public Data Dump

Evidence and journals stay local unless deliberately summarized safely.

Manna-MarketWatch is one of the clearest examples of Manna's truthfulness rule: interesting patterns do not matter until they survive evidence, cost, baseline, and readiness gates.
